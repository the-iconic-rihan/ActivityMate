# Launch City Playbook Mumbai

## Overview

This playbook sequences ActivityMate's Mumbai launch neighborhood by neighborhood, against the primary persona: the relocated urban professional aged 21–40 (new employee, transferee, or recent graduate) who has disposable time and income but no trusted local circle yet. It operationalizes the organizer-led growth loop from [growth-strategy-loops.md](growth-strategy-loops.md) into a concrete week-by-week plan for the first 90 days.

## Why Mumbai, and Why This Sequence

Mumbai was chosen (see [../02-market-research/city-expansion-readiness-scoring.md](../02-market-research/city-expansion-readiness-scoring.md)) for its density of relocated professionals concentrated in a small number of commercial and residential clusters, its high smartphone/UPI penetration, and its acute version of the "no one to go with" problem — long commutes and demanding work hours fragment the informal social networks people would otherwise rely on. Rather than launching city-wide on day one, we sequence neighborhood by neighborhood so that early density is concentrated enough for activities to actually fill, matching the cold-start logic in the growth loops document.

## Neighborhood Sequencing

### Wave 1 (Weeks 1–4): Lower Parel and Bandra-Kurla Complex (BKC)

These are the highest-density clusters of the primary persona: corporate offices (BKC — finance, consulting, media) and mixed office/residential (Lower Parel — IT, agencies, co-working). Commute patterns mean many residents of Bandra West, Khar, and Prabhadevi work in one of these two clusters, so early activities anchored here have a built-in evening and weekend audience without needing to solve residential density separately.

- **Launch-week tactics:** Founding-host recruitment concentrated at 5–8 co-working spaces and cafes identified in [partnership-strategy.md](partnership-strategy.md); in-venue QR posters and a "first activity free host support" offer (growth team personally helps a founding host fill their first activity via targeted invites).
- **Category focus:** After-work categories that fit a commute-tired professional — coffee, dinner, badminton, coworking meetups, weekend cycling. Trekking and weekend-trip categories are seeded but not pushed until Wave 2, once a critical mass of trust-scored users exists to fill smaller, higher-commitment groups.

### Wave 2 (Weeks 5–8): Powai and Andheri (including Chakala/SEEPZ corridor)

Powai combines IT/product company campuses with a large student population (IIT Bombay and surrounding institutes), giving a second persona segment — students and young professionals — inside a single walkable geography around Powai Lake. Andheri East/West adds another dense professional corridor plus proximity to the airport (useful for the solo-traveller persona).

- **Launch-week tactics:** Cross-promote from Wave 1 hosts who have Powai/Andheri connections (colleagues who transferred campuses, friends). Run a "bring your building" push targeting large residential complexes (Hiranandani Gardens, Powai's high-rise clusters) where new-mover density is high.
- **Category focus:** Running and photography-walk categories around Powai Lake; museum and networking categories leveraging the professional density in Andheri.

### Wave 3 (Weeks 9–12): South Mumbai (Colaba, Fort, Marine Drive) and Thane

South Mumbai adds the weekend leisure and heritage-activity persona (museum visits, photography walks, evening walks along Marine Drive) and a wealthier, slightly older sub-segment. Thane is included as the first suburban/satellite extension, testing whether the model transfers beyond core commercial Mumbai to the wider metropolitan region — an early signal for later city expansion (see [../41-localization-expansion/](../41-localization-expansion/)).

- **Launch-week tactics:** Partnership-anchored activities at heritage/cultural venues rather than pure cafe seeding; South Mumbai's persona responds better to a curated single flagship activity (e.g., a photography walk from Kala Ghoda to Marine Drive) than to volume seeding.
- **Category focus:** Museum, photography walk, board games (leveraging South Mumbai's cafe culture), and the first weekend-trip pilots (day trips to nearby destinations like Karnala or Kihim, testing the higher-trust, higher-commitment activity category before rolling it out city-wide).

## Persona-to-Neighborhood Mapping

| Persona | Primary neighborhoods | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Relocated corporate professional | BKC, Lower Parel, Andheri | Office density, evening free time, disposable income |
| Student / recent graduate | Powai, Vile Parle/Kalina corridor (Wave 4 candidate) | University density, lower cost sensitivity, high social appetite |
| Solo traveller / short-stay | Andheri (airport proximity), South Mumbai (tourist/heritage draw) | Transient population needing immediate, low-commitment activities |
| Recently relocated family | Powai, Thane | Residential complex density, weekend-oriented categories |

## Launch-Week Playbook (repeatable per wave)

1. **T-14 days:** Identify and personally recruit 15–20 founding-host candidates in the target neighborhood via warm intros, LinkedIn outreach, and partner venues.
2. **T-7 days:** Founding hosts complete verification (see [../13-trust-safety-fraud/user-verification-levels.md](../13-trust-safety-fraud/user-verification-levels.md)) and get 1:1 onboarding calls; first activities are scheduled for launch week itself so the app never shows an empty activity feed to a new arrival.
3. **T-0 (Launch day):** Venue partner activation (posters, staff mentions), local Instagram geo-targeted posts, and a founder/growth-lead presence at at least one flagship activity to seed word-of-mouth.
4. **T+7 days:** Review RSVP-to-attendance ratio and ratings per host; underperforming activities get direct growth-team support rather than being left to fail publicly.
5. **T+30 days:** Gate decision — only expand paid spend (per [growth-strategy-loops.md](growth-strategy-loops.md)) in this neighborhood if 3+ consecutive weeks show activities at 60%+ of stated capacity.
