# ASO — App Store Optimization

## Overview

App store search is a meaningful discovery channel for ActivityMate because it captures high-intent users at the exact moment they're looking for a solution — "activity partner," "trek group Mumbai," "people to hang out with" — rather than passively scrolling. Because the product is easy to mis-categorize as a dating app by both users and app-store algorithms, ASO here has to actively fight category confusion, not just optimize for volume keywords.

## Context / Problem

If ActivityMate is discovered and installed by users looking for a dating app, two bad outcomes follow: high uninstall/low-completion rates that hurt the store ranking algorithm, and a trickle of users onboarding with the wrong expectations, which is itself a moderation and trust-safety load (mismatched intent is one of the more common early-stage report categories in comparable platforms). ASO strategy therefore has to optimize for *qualified* installs, not raw install volume.

## Keyword Strategy

- **Primary keywords (high intent, low ambiguity):** "activity partner," "find trek group," "Mumbai meetup app," "activity buddy," "group activities Mumbai," "verified meetup app."
- **Secondary keywords (category-specific, tied to the initial activity categories in PRD-001 §8):** "badminton partner Mumbai," "coworking meetup," "trekking group Mumbai," "running partner app," "board games meetup."
- **Deliberately avoided or suppressed:** generic "dating," "singles," "match" terms, and anything that would rank the app alongside Bumble/Tinder/Hinge in store "similar apps" surfacing. Where App Store/Play Store category selection allows it, ActivityMate is filed under Lifestyle/Social rather than Dating, with category-defining screenshots and description copy reinforcing that placement.

## Store Listing Guidelines

- **App title/subtitle:** Leads with the activity-first framing (e.g., "ActivityMate — Real Activities, Real People"), never with language that could be read as a dating hook.
- **Screenshots:** First three screenshots (the ones that actually get seen before a scroll) show the verification/trust-score UI, an activity listing (e.g., a trek or badminton meetup), and a group chat — in that order, so the trust mechanic is the first thing a browsing user sees, ahead of any social/chat UI that could be misread as a dating flow.
- **Description copy:** Opens with the "not a dating app" framing directly and briefly (consistent with [../33-branding-identity/brand-positioning-statement.md](../33-branding-identity/brand-positioning-statement.md)), because category confusion is a discovery-quality problem worth spending the first line of copy to prevent.
- **Reviews/ratings prompts:** Triggered only after a completed, rated activity (not at generic engagement milestones), so store ratings correlate with actual product delivery rather than onboarding polish alone.

## Localization Note

Store listing copy ships in English first for Mumbai launch, with Hindi and Marathi keyword variants evaluated in a fast-follow pass once English listing performance data exists — premature localization without conversion data would be guessing rather than optimization.

## Recommendation & Rationale

ASO priority is qualified-install rate and category-correct discovery over raw keyword volume, mirroring the "safety over growth" principle applied to a store-ranking context: an app store algorithm rewards retention and engagement, and mis-targeted installs actively hurt both, so precise targeting is also the higher-ranking strategy, not just the safer one.
