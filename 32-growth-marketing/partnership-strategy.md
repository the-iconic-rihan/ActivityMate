# Partnership Strategy

## Overview

Venue partnerships serve two functions simultaneously: they are a launch marketing channel (visibility, credibility, founding-host recruitment surface) and they become the seed inventory for the verified-venue list that [../17-maps-location/meetup-point-safety-recommendations.md](../17-maps-location/meetup-point-safety-recommendations.md) draws from when recommending safe, pre-vetted meeting points to users. Treating these as one workstream rather than two separate efforts (marketing partnerships vs. safety-vetted locations) avoids building duplicate vetting processes.

## Context / Problem

Cold-start activities need a default answer to "where do we meet" that doesn't rely on an unverified host picking an arbitrary address. Public venues — cafes, coworking spaces, sports facilities — solve this well: they're public, staffed, have natural foot traffic, and give both host and guest a neutral, low-risk first-meeting location (this is also a direct input to trust-safety's meetup-point guidance, not just a marketing nicety). At the same time, ActivityMate needs physical-world visibility in the neighborhoods it's launching into, and cafes/coworking spaces are exactly where the primary persona already spends time.

## Partner Categories and Selection Criteria

### Cafes and casual dining

Primary partners for the coffee, breakfast, lunch, and dinner categories. Selection criteria: consistent daytime and evening footfall, seating that accommodates small groups (4–8) without a mandatory large-table minimum spend, staff willingness to display a QR/poster, and — critically for the safety-inventory function — good lighting, a public street-facing location, and reasonably reliable opening hours (a venue that closes unpredictably is a poor safety-recommendation default).

### Coworking spaces

Primary partners for coworking/networking categories and a strong founding-host recruitment channel, since coworking members already self-select for the relocated-professional persona. Selection criteria: a community/events manager we can build a direct relationship with (this person is often already running informal meetups and is a strong founding-host candidate), day-pass or guest-access policy that doesn't block non-members from attending an ActivityMate activity there.

### Sports and recreation facilities

Badminton courts, running clubs' regular meeting points, cycling shop/rental partners. Selection criteria: bookable court/facility availability that a host can reserve as part of creating an activity (a future integration point noted for [../15-activities-communities/activity-lifecycle-spec.md](../15-activities-communities/activity-lifecycle-spec.md)), safe and well-lit access particularly for early-morning or evening slots.

### Weekend-trip and trekking operators

A smaller, more curated tier — local trekking groups and day-trip operators near Mumbai (e.g., routes toward Karnala, Kihim, Matheran) who can be vetted more rigorously (background-check-adjacent, per [../13-trust-safety-fraud/background-check-partnership-evaluation.md](../13-trust-safety-fraud/background-check-partnership-evaluation.md)) given the higher-commitment, higher-risk nature of overnight or remote-location activities.

## Vetting Process

Every candidate venue goes through: (1) a physical visit by the growth/ops team, (2) a safety checklist (public visibility, lighting, staffed hours, easy exit routes) co-owned with trust-safety, (3) a written partnership agreement covering signage/QR placement and any referral or discount terms, (4) addition to the shared verified-venue list consumed by both this document's launch tactics and maps-location's recommendation engine. This shared vetting step is the reason the folder dependency runs partnership-strategy → maps-location rather than the two lists being maintained independently.

## Sequencing Against Launch Waves

Partnership recruitment runs one wave ahead of the neighborhood launch schedule in [launch-city-playbook-mumbai.md](launch-city-playbook-mumbai.md): Lower Parel/BKC venues are signed by T-21 days of Wave 1, Powai/Andheri venues by T-21 days of Wave 2, and so on, so that founding hosts in each new neighborhood have a ready-made venue list on day one rather than having to find their own locations.

## Recommendation & Rationale

Run venue partnerships as a single unified workstream feeding both marketing visibility and the safety-vetted meetup-point inventory, vetted against a shared safety-first checklist rather than a marketing-only checklist. This costs more per-venue effort up front than a pure marketing partnership push would, but it means every launch partner is already usable as a recommended safe meeting point, with no second vetting pass needed later.
