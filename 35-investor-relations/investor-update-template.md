# Investor Update Template

**Status:** Draft v1.

## Scope

A monthly or quarterly investor-update format.

## Purpose and cadence

Regular investor updates — sent whether or not there's good news — are one of the highest-leverage, lowest-cost trust-building habits a founding team can maintain with its cap table. Recommended cadence: **monthly** for the first 18 months (when the company is moving fastest and investors have the least other visibility), stepping to **quarterly** once a board is formally seated and the [board-deck-template.md](board-deck-template.md) cadence takes over the deeper review function. This template is deliberately shorter and more informal than the board deck — a 5-minute read, sent by email, not a meeting.

## Template

```
Subject: ActivityMate — [Month/Quarter] Update

Hi all,

Quick update on ActivityMate for [period].

HEADLINE
[One or two sentences: the single most important thing that happened this period —
good or bad. Do not bury bad news in the middle of the email.]

METRICS
- WAU: [x] (vs. [x] last period)
- Weekly completed trusted meetups per active user (north star): [x]
- Activity completion rate: [x]%
- Female retention: [x]%
- Cash position / runway: ₹[x] / [x] months

WHAT WENT WELL
- [bullet]
- [bullet]

WHAT DIDN'T GO WELL
- [bullet — genuine, specific problems, not softened]

TRUST & SAFETY
- [Any incidents, or "no incidents this period" — this section is never omitted]

WHAT'S NEXT
- [Top 2-3 priorities for next period]

THE ASK
- [Specific things investors can help with: intros, hires, expertise — or
  "nothing needed this period" if genuinely true]

Thanks,
[Founder name]
```

## Content principles

1. **Bad news travels first, not last.** If the north-star metric is flat or down, or a safety incident occurred, that goes in the headline — not softened into "what didn't go well" three sections down. Investors who learn about problems late lose trust faster than investors who learn about problems early.
2. **Metrics are always the same five to seven numbers, every period.** Consistency lets investors build their own trend line across updates without re-reading old emails. The metric set should mirror [north-star-kpi-dashboard-spec.md](../22-analytics-experimentation/north-star-kpi-dashboard-spec.md) so there is exactly one definition of each number across every investor-facing document.
3. **The trust & safety line is never skipped**, even to write "no incidents this period" — silence on this section reads as evasion, not good news, to an investor who knows this is a trust-first company.
4. **The ask is specific.** "Let us know if you can help" gets ignored. "Do you have a warm intro to a Series A lead who has backed a trust/marketplace business in India?" gets acted on.

## Ownership

Drafted by the CEO/founders each cycle, ideally within 5 business days of period close. Should reuse the same metrics pulled for [board-deck-template.md](board-deck-template.md) rather than recalculating them separately, to avoid two slightly different numbers circulating among the same investor group.
