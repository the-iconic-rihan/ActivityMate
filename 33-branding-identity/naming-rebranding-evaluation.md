# Naming & Rebranding Evaluation

## Overview

"ActivityMate" is the working name used throughout this repository, marked explicitly in PRD-001 as "(Working Name)." This document sets the process and criteria for resolving that placeholder to a final brand name before public launch, and the interim rules for using "ActivityMate" safely until that decision is made.

## Context / Problem

The working name has a structural weakness worth naming directly: "-mate" suffixes are heavily associated with dating and companionship apps in the Indian and global app-store landscape, which cuts against the "not a dating app" positioning this entire repository is built around (see [brand-positioning-statement.md](brand-positioning-statement.md)). It is serviceable as an internal working name — descriptive, easy for the team to say — but carries real category-confusion risk if it ships as the permanent public name, particularly for ASO discovery (see [../32-growth-marketing/aso-app-store-optimization.md](../32-growth-marketing/aso-app-store-optimization.md)) where "mate" adjacent to activity/social keywords could pull in dating-app algorithmic association.

## Options Considered

### Option A — Keep "ActivityMate" as the permanent name

- **Pros:** Zero rebranding cost, no re-registration of trademarks/domains/app-store listings, team and early-user familiarity carries straight through to public launch, descriptive name requires no explanation of what the product does.
- **Cons:** Carries the "-mate" dating-adjacent connotation risk noted above. Descriptive names are also harder to trademark cleanly and harder to differentiate in a crowded app-store category once competitors exist.

### Option B — Resolve to a new name before public launch

- **Pros:** Removes the category-confusion risk at its root, gives the brand a clean trademark and domain position, and allows the name itself to reinforce positioning (trust, real-world, activity) rather than needing tagline copy to do all that work.
- **Cons:** Real cost — trademark search and registration, domain acquisition, app-store listing history reset (early reviews/installs under the old name don't automatically transfer ASO equity), and a re-briefing of every downstream document and asset that currently says "ActivityMate."

### Option C — Keep "ActivityMate" through Mumbai launch, resolve after

- **Pros:** Defers the cost and risk of a rename until after there's real usage data on whether category confusion is an actual problem (measurable via mis-onboarding rate and dating-app-expectation support tickets, see [../34-operations-support/customer-support-playbook.md](../34-operations-support/customer-support-playbook.md)) rather than a hypothetical one. Avoids spending scarce pre-launch time on a naming process instead of product and trust-safety work, which are harder to defer.
- **Cons:** Risks anchoring the brand to a name the team and early users get attached to, making a later rename feel like a bigger disruption than it would have been pre-launch.

## Recommendation & Rationale

Option C. Launch Mumbai under "ActivityMate," but treat the name as explicitly provisional in all legal, trademark, and domain filings (avoid exclusive long-term commitments where possible), and run a structured naming evaluation in parallel with the first 90 days of launch data from [../32-growth-marketing/launch-city-playbook-mumbai.md](../32-growth-marketing/launch-city-playbook-mumbai.md). The decision gate is: if post-launch data shows measurable category confusion (tracked via onboarding drop-off tied to dating-app expectation and support-ticket tagging), the rename moves from "P1, pre-Series-A" to urgent. If confusion is low, the cost of a rename may not be justified and Option A becomes the de facto outcome.

## Naming Criteria for the Evaluation Process

Any candidate replacement name is scored against:

1. **No dating/romantic connotation** — the primary criterion, directly derived from the positioning statement.
2. **Trust/safety association or neutrality** — bonus for names that evoke reliability, real-world presence, or community, without overclaiming safety in a way that becomes a liability if a real incident occurs.
3. **Trademark and domain availability in India** (and app-store name availability) — checked before any name reaches shortlist status.
4. **Pronounceable and spellable across Hindi/English/Marathi contexts** — Mumbai launch requires a name that transliterates cleanly.
5. **Not overly narrow to one activity category** — the name should not imply, e.g., only trekking or only dining, given the multi-category MVP scope (PRD-001 §8).

## Process

Growth and founders run a shortlisting pass (10–15 candidates) against the criteria above, legal counsel runs trademark/domain clearance on the top 5, and a small user-research pass (see [../03-user-research/](../03-user-research/)) tests top 2–3 candidates for connotation and pronounceability with the Mumbai target persona before a final founder decision.
