# Marketing Asset Guidelines

## Overview

This document sets the templates and visual rules marketing uses for social and paid channels, built on top of the visual tokens and components already defined in [../06-design-system/](../06-design-system/) rather than inventing a parallel visual language. It exists because marketing assets are produced faster and by more hands (freelancers, agencies, growth team) than the product UI, so drift from brand and positioning happens fastest here if it isn't constrained explicitly.

## Foundational Rule

Every marketing asset pulls its color palette, typography, and iconography from [../06-design-system/typography-color-system.md](../06-design-system/typography-color-system.md) and [../06-design-system/iconography-illustration-guidelines.md](../06-design-system/iconography-illustration-guidelines.md). Marketing does not introduce a separate "campaign palette" — visual consistency between the ad a user clicks and the app they land in is itself a trust signal (an inconsistent bait-and-switch look is a classic scam-app pattern we actively want to avoid resembling).

## Asset Templates

### Social (organic — Instagram/feed and story formats)

Standard templates: (1) **Activity spotlight** — real photo from a completed activity, host name and trust-score badge visible, category tag, no romantic-pairing crop or composition (per [brand-positioning-statement.md](brand-positioning-statement.md)); (2) **Safety-literacy carousel** — 4–6 slide format for content like "how verification works," using design-system iconography rather than stock safety/shield clip-art, which reads as generic and slightly alarming; (3) **Host spotlight** — portrait-orientation, quote-led, supports the founding-host recruitment motion from [../32-growth-marketing/growth-strategy-loops.md](../32-growth-marketing/growth-strategy-loops.md).

### Paid (performance — Meta/Google)

Paid creative is held to a stricter rule than organic: **no creative may use romantic pairing imagery, sunset-couple framing, or copy patterns associated with dating-app ads**, even where those patterns are proven high-performers in the broader social/lifestyle app category. This is a deliberate performance tradeoff — some romantic-adjacent creative patterns likely convert well in isolation, but they directly undercut the positioning this repo is built on and would need to be walked back publicly if flagged. Paid creative instead leans on the activity-first spotlight template and a clear, single-line value proposition ("Verified people. Real activities. Mumbai.").

### Venue/partner co-branded assets

Templates for QR posters and in-venue signage used in [../32-growth-marketing/partnership-strategy.md](../32-growth-marketing/partnership-strategy.md) launch tactics — venue logo placement is secondary and clearly subordinate to the ActivityMate mark, with a consistent "Hosted here" or "Verified meetup spot" tag line that reinforces the trust-inventory function these venues serve.

## Do / Don't

- Do use real, consented photos from completed activities wherever possible; don't use generic stock photography of unrelated group-of-friends imagery that could be mistaken for staged/fake social proof.
- Do show diverse, mixed-gender group activity contexts that reflect the actual product experience (multiple people, activity-focused framing); don't show two-person, romantically-framed compositions.
- Do keep trust-score/verification badges visible in spotlight assets where the underlying activity had them; don't retouch or omit safety UI elements to make an asset "cleaner," since that misrepresents the product.

## Approval & Governance

Any asset intended for paid spend or press use is checked against this document and [brand-positioning-statement.md](brand-positioning-statement.md) before publishing; organic social has a lighter-weight self-check against the do/don't list above, escalating to Head of Marketing/Design review only for new template types, not for every individual post.
