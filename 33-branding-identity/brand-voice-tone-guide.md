# Brand Voice & Tone Guide

## Overview

Tone is a trust signal on ActivityMate in a way it isn't for most consumer apps. A platform that asks strangers to meet up in person cannot sound flippant about safety without undermining its own core promise, but it also cannot sound like a compliance department without killing the warmth that makes people want to actually use it for coffee, treks, and badminton. This guide sets the operating rule — warm, but never casual to the point of feeling unsafe — and gives concrete do/don't phrasing so marketing, product copy, and community management stay consistent with [brand-positioning-statement.md](brand-positioning-statement.md).

## The Core Rule

Every piece of copy should sound like it was written by someone who has personally organized meetups for years, knows most first-meetings go fine, and also knows exactly what to do on the rare occasion something doesn't — and writes with the calm competence of that experience, not the anxiety of a legal disclaimer and not the breeziness of an events-listing app that has never had to think about it.

## Voice Attributes

- **Warm, not chatty.** We write like a knowledgeable local friend, not a hype-driven growth app. We avoid excessive exclamation points, forced slang, and "vibes"-driven copy that would read as unserious next to a safety feature.
- **Direct, not clinical.** Safety and trust mechanics are explained in plain language, not legal or corporate hedging. If verification is required, we say why in one sentence, not in a paragraph of liability language.
- **Confident, not authoritative-cold.** We state what the platform does ("Every host is phone- and selfie-verified before their first activity") rather than what users must comply with ("Users are required to complete verification prior to activity creation").
- **Encouraging, not pressuring.** We invite participation without urgency tactics. No countdown-timer language, no "only 2 spots left!" framing on categories where that pressure could push someone into a decision they'd otherwise vet more carefully (see the deliberate avoidance of FOMO mechanics in [../32-growth-marketing/content-marketing-strategy.md](../32-growth-marketing/content-marketing-strategy.md)).

## Do / Don't Examples

**Verification prompt**
- Do: "Quick step before you host: verify your phone and take a selfie. It takes two minutes and it's what keeps ActivityMate a place people trust."
- Don't: "Verification required. Please complete KYC steps to proceed." *(too clinical — sounds like a bank, not a community)*
- Don't: "Get verified and start meeting amazing new people today!! 🎉" *(too breezy for a safety-gating step — undersells why it matters)*

**Trust score explanation**
- Do: "Your trust score reflects how reliably you show up and how others rate their time with you. It grows the more you participate — and it's visible to anyone deciding whether to join your activity."
- Don't: "Users are scored on a proprietary trust algorithm based on multiple weighted factors." *(opaque, sounds like it's hiding something — contradicts [../13-trust-safety-fraud/trust-score-transparency-policy.md](../13-trust-safety-fraud/trust-score-transparency-policy.md))*

**Safety incident / report flow**
- Do: "If something felt off during an activity, tell us. Reports are reviewed by a real person, and we take them seriously — always."
- Don't: "Report inappropriate behavior using the button below." *(true but cold — misses the reassurance this moment needs)*
- Don't: "Don't worry, everything will be fine!" *(false reassurance — never promise an outcome we can't guarantee)*

**Cancellation / no-show**
- Do: "Plans change — just let your group know as early as you can. Repeated late cancellations affect your trust score, since reliability is what makes this work for everyone."
- Don't: "Cancelling activities may result in penalties." *(threat-first framing where explanation-first framing builds more compliance)*

**Marketing / activity promotion copy**
- Do: "Sunday trek to Karnala — six spots, all verified hosts and hikers. First time? You'll get a pre-trip safety checklist and a group chat before you go."
- Don't: "Meet cute new people on a weekend adventure 😍" *(romantic framing — directly violates the "not a dating app" positioning)*

## Tone Calibration by Context

| Context | Tone lean | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Onboarding / verification | Direct, reassuring | Explain the "why," never skip it |
| Activity discovery/browse | Warm, inviting | Closest to conventional consumer-app tone, but never romantic framing |
| Trust score / ratings | Transparent, plain-language | No algorithmic mystique |
| Safety reports / incidents | Calm, serious, human | Never clinical, never dismissive |
| Crisis / press-facing | Measured, accountable | Governed separately by [../34-operations-support/crisis-communication-plan.md](../34-operations-support/crisis-communication-plan.md), but same underlying voice |
